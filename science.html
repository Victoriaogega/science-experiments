Experiment one : Balloon propelled boat

Materials needed

Styrofoam

Balloons

Straws

What the machine solves

The machine solves the issue of propulsion without using electricity.
It explains how pressure released in a controlled manner can power up a boat and many
other things. It explains energy transfer, force and motion.

Energy efficiency and user-centred design

The design emphasizes simplicity,reusability and accessibility.
It is made from materials that are easily available and affordable which are easily accessible.
From a human-centred perspective and easy and safe to make.The energy efficiency is about 7.5.

Mechanical principles involved

Styrofoam is a material that can float on water due to its low density . Its lightweight and porous
structure allows it to displace water that is equal to its volume, resulting in the buoyant force needed for it to float.
We connected it to a balloon using a straw. The balloon is attached at the back of the boat . Once the balloon is
deflated and the boat placed on water, it starts to move. As the balloon deflates, the force pushes the boat to the
opposite direction because the compressed air rushes out through the straw. As the air rapidly rushes out, it creates
a force that pushes the boat on water. This is based on Newton's third law of motion which states that for every action,
there is an equal and opposite reaction. The balloon acts as the propulsion system for the boat; when it is inflated, it stores
a large amount of air under pressure which is potential energy. The potential energy is converted to kinetic energy when the
air is released and it propels the boat to the opposite direction.

Experiment two : Board game

Focus topic : Genetics

Game Flow


Each player gets 4 rabbit cards at the start of each game. One game consists of five to seven rounds. Each round has 4 phases which are:

1. Event Phase

Draw 1 Event Card. All players read and apply the effect (e.g., “Cold Snap” may cause some rabbits to die). Roll the die if required to determine the impact.

2. Survival Check

Some events will cause specific traits (e.g., “Thick Fur”) to help rabbits survive. Rabbits without helpful traits may die based on the event or die roll.

3.Reproduction Phase

Colonies can only reproduce if they have enough space.
You may set a max of 8 rabbits per colony.

4. Drift Phase

This simulates genetic drift (random loss in a small population). This is where rare traits may disappear by chance.
